# Final Recommendation

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

Based on the regression analysis, the variables most strongly associated with monthly sales are:

* **Footfall**
* **Marketing Spend**
* **Inventory Availability**

The multiple regression model indicates that all three variables have positive coefficients and statistically significant p-values (less than 0.05), suggesting a strong association with monthly sales.

---

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

The leadership team should prioritize the following areas:

* Increase customer footfall through targeted marketing campaigns and promotional activities.
* Invest marketing budgets efficiently, as higher marketing spend is associated with higher sales.
* Improve inventory availability to reduce stock-outs and ensure products remain available for customers.

These variables showed the strongest relationship with monthly sales and are likely to have the greatest business impact.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Regional dummy variables should be interpreted carefully.

* **Region South** and **Region West** showed statistically significant positive effects compared to the reference category (East).
* **Region North** was not statistically significant (p-value > 0.05), indicating that its estimated effect may be due to random variation.

Regional differences may also reflect other business conditions such as customer demographics, local competition, or store characteristics that are not included in the model.

---

## Business Recommendation

The multiple regression model explains approximately **81.14%** of the variation in monthly sales (R² = **0.8114**), making it the most reliable model among those evaluated.

Based on the analysis, the company should:

* Increase investment in marketing activities that drive customer traffic.
* Focus on improving customer footfall across stores.
* Maintain high inventory availability to support sales growth.
* Use regional insights to support local business strategies rather than making nationwide decisions based solely on location.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Although the regression model performs well, several limitations should be considered:

* Regression identifies statistical relationships but cannot confirm cause-and-effect.
* Important business factors such as competitor promotions, economic conditions, seasonal demand, and customer preferences were not included in the model.
* Future business conditions may differ from the historical data used for model development.

Therefore, business decisions should combine regression insights with operational knowledge and additional analysis.

---

## Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis measures the strength of association between variables.

A significant relationship does **not** necessarily mean that one variable directly causes another. For example, higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales, but both could also be influenced by other factors such as seasonal demand or business strategy.

Therefore, regression results should be used to guide business decisions while recognizing that controlled experiments and additional analyses are required to establish true causal relationships.

---

## Final Conclusion

The multiple regression model is recommended for business decision-making because it provides the highest explanatory power (R² = **0.8114**) and identifies marketing spend, footfall, and inventory availability as the strongest drivers of monthly sales.

Leadership should focus on improving these key business drivers while interpreting regional effects carefully and acknowledging that regression analysis shows association rather than definitive causation.
